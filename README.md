## Analytical Questions / Problems to Solve
- Which coffee type generates the most consistent revenue over time?
- Are there seasonal patterns in coffee sales across years?
- Which countries contribute most to total revenue, and how concentrated is revenue geographically?
- How dependent is revenue on a small group of top customers?
- Do loyalty card holders significantly impact overall sales volume?
- Which product sizes and roast types should be prioritized for inventory optimization?

The primary objective is to support data-driven decision-making in pricing, customer segmentation, inventory planning, and market expansion

## Analysis & Insights
1. Sales Trends Over Time
- Sales show high volatility month-to-month, indicating sensitivity to seasonality or promotions.
- Peak sales occur intermittently, particularly mid-year and toward year-end.
- Arabica and Liberica show higher peaks, suggesting stronger customer preference or pricing power.

2. Coffee Type Performance
- Arabica consistently shows strong performance with higher peaks.
- Robusta appears more volatile with lower average revenue.
- Excelsa and Liberica show niche but periodic spikes.

3. Sales by Country
- The United States dominates revenue, accounting for the majority of total sales.
- Ireland and the UK contribute significantly less, indicating market concentration risk.
- Insight: Revenue is geographically imbalanced.

## Business Recommendations
- Prioritize Arabica production and inventory: Highest and most consistent revenue driver.
- Expand geographically beyond the U.S.: Reduce revenue concentration risk by targeting Ireland/UK growth.
- Strengthen loyalty programs: Use loyalty data to push targeted promotions and subscription models.
- Introduce seasonal campaigns: Align promotions with observed peak demand periods.
